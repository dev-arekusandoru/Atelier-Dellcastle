About

Growing up in Romania I had little opportunity for artistic expression until my later teens. Since then I have discovered multiple avenues for creativity including cooking, baking and music. My love of woodworking developed after moving into a "fixer-upper" house. My skills and selection of tools grew as I worked and I discovered the beauty of hand tools. I went from reading about home repair to “Fine Woodworking.” Various small tables, benches and stools allowed me to practice joinery techniques. In 2018 I crafted a tea box with internal dividers and a book-matched lid as a gift for my wife. That project was the spark that ignited my love of box making and fine woodworking.

Each piece is one of a kind. I use miter joints, splines, finger joints, or jig cut dovetails. Beginning with rough sawn lumber, I joint, plane and resaw based on the design needs of each project. I use hand scrapers, planes, Japanese hand saws, and chisels in my projects. Handles are designed and hand cut using scroll saw, drill press, band saw, or chisel. Dividers for jewelry and tea boxes are compression fit. My favorite part of crafting dividers is bird’s mouth joinery using a combination of router and shooting plane. I also really enjoy scroll saw work and developed a technique for creating .25” inlays. You will find my inlay work in serving boards, lazy susans and coasters.

To explore some of the designs I have made, check out the Gallery. To commission a piece, please reach out through the Contact page. Visit the Shows page to see where I will be displaying and selling some of my work.
